# Guidance for AI Adoption (AI6) — October 2025

Source: Australian Government Department of Industry, Science and Resources (October 2025)

The AI6 framework supersedes the earlier Voluntary AI Safety Standard (September 2024) and condenses Australia's AI governance expectations into 6 essential practices. Supporting tools include an AI screening tool, AI policy guide and template, and AI register template.

## Context: Australia's Regulatory Approach

On 2 December 2025, the National AI Plan confirmed the Government **will not proceed with mandatory guardrails** for AI. Instead, Australia adopted a "technology-neutral" approach relying on existing laws (Privacy Act, Consumer Law, Copyright Act, workplace laws, sector-specific regulation).

An **AI Safety Institute** was announced (November 2025) with $29.9 million in funding to test AI systems, assess risks, and recommend targeted reforms.

The Productivity Commission estimated AI could contribute **AUD $116 billion** over the next decade.

## The 6 Essential Practices

### Practice 1: Know Your AI

**Objective:** Maintain visibility over all AI systems in use within the organisation.

**Requirements:**
- Maintain an AI register listing all AI tools, their purposes, data inputs, and risk levels
- Include both approved AND known Shadow AI tools
- Record data flows: what data goes in, where it's processed, who has access
- Update the register when new tools are adopted or existing ones change

**For SMEs:** At minimum, maintain a spreadsheet listing:
- Tool name and provider
- What it's used for
- What data it accesses
- Where data is processed (Australia/overseas)
- Whether it's officially approved
- Risk rating (Low/Medium/High/Critical)

### Practice 2: Be Accountable

**Objective:** Establish clear accountability for AI governance outcomes.

**Requirements:**
- Designate named individuals responsible for AI governance
- Define roles: who approves new AI tools, who responds to incidents, who conducts reviews
- Ensure accountability extends to third-party AI tools, not just internally developed systems
- Board/management awareness of AI risks and obligations

**For SMEs:** Assign one person as AI Governance Contact. They don't need to be a specialist — they need to be empowered to make decisions about AI tool approval, incident response, and policy compliance.

### Practice 3: Understand and Manage Risks

**Objective:** Identify, assess, and mitigate risks from AI adoption.

**Requirements:**
- Conduct risk assessments before deploying AI systems
- Consider: accuracy, bias, privacy, security, safety, legal compliance
- Assess proportionally — higher-risk AI applications require deeper assessment
- Include risks from AI tool supply chain (vendor data handling, model updates)

**Key AI Risks for SMEs:**
1. **Data breach** through external AI tools (NDB scheme)
2. **Privacy non-compliance** (APPs, especially APP 6, 8, 11)
3. **Inaccurate outputs** leading to poor business/customer decisions
4. **IP exposure** through AI tool training on business data
5. **Bias/discrimination** in AI-assisted decisions
6. **Regulatory penalties** (up to $50M under POLA Act)
7. **Statutory tort** claims for privacy invasion (up to $478,550)
8. **ACL violations** from misleading AI-generated content

### Practice 4: Be Transparent

**Objective:** Ensure stakeholders know when and how AI is used.

**Requirements:**
- Disclose AI use to individuals significantly affected by AI decisions
- Update privacy policies to reflect AI processing (mandatory by December 2026 for automated decisions)
- Inform customers when interacting with AI systems (chatbots, automated responses)
- Be transparent with employees about AI monitoring and AI-assisted HR decisions

**Regulatory Mapping:**
- APP 1.7–1.9 (POLA Act): mandatory disclosure of automated decision-making by Dec 2026
- AI Ethics Principle 6: Transparency and Explainability
- ACL s18: misleading conduct includes failing to disclose material AI involvement

### Practice 5: Prioritise Safety and Fairness

**Objective:** Ensure AI systems are reliable, safe, and don't produce unfair outcomes.

**Requirements:**
- Test AI outputs for accuracy before relying on them for decisions
- Monitor for bias, especially in decisions affecting individuals
- Implement human oversight for high-stakes AI decisions
- Ensure AI systems don't discriminate based on protected attributes

**For SMEs:**
- Always have a human review AI-generated decisions before acting on them
- Be especially careful with AI-assisted hiring, lending, insurance, and pricing
- Document your testing and oversight processes
- Don't use AI for decisions that require professional qualifications (legal, medical, financial advice)

### Practice 6: Engage, Consult, and Review

**Objective:** Continuously improve AI governance through stakeholder engagement and regular review.

**Requirements:**
- Engage with stakeholders (staff, customers, regulators) about AI use
- Review AI policies at least annually or when significant changes occur
- Learn from incidents and near-misses
- Stay current with regulatory developments (POLA Act Tranche 2, OAIC guidance updates)
- Share lessons learned across the organisation

**For SMEs:**
- Schedule annual AI policy reviews
- Conduct post-incident reviews when AI issues arise
- Survey staff about AI usage patterns (discover Shadow AI)
- Monitor regulatory announcements from OAIC and Attorney-General's Department

## AI6 Compliance Scorecard for SMEs

| Practice | Key Question | Minimum Requirement |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Know Your AI | Do you maintain an AI register? | List of all AI tools with data flows |
| Be Accountable | Is someone named as AI governance lead? | Designated contact with authority |
| Manage Risks | Have you assessed AI risks? | PIA or risk assessment conducted |
| Be Transparent | Does your privacy policy cover AI? | Automated decision disclosure (by Dec 2026) |
| Safety & Fairness | Is there human oversight of AI decisions? | Review process for AI outputs |
| Engage & Review | When did you last review AI policies? | Annual review scheduled |
