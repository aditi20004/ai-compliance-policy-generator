# Australia's 8 AI Ethics Principles

Source: Australian Government Department of Industry, Science and Resources

These voluntary principles guide the responsible design, development, and implementation of AI in Australia.

## Principle 1: Human, Societal and Environmental Wellbeing

AI systems should benefit individuals, society, and the environment. Throughout their lifecycle, AI systems should be used in ways that are beneficial to individuals, society, and the environment. Designers, developers, and users should consider the impacts on current and future generations and take a precautionary approach.

## Principle 2: Human-Centred Values

AI systems should respect human rights, diversity, and the autonomy of individuals. Throughout their lifecycle, AI systems should respect human rights, diversity, and the autonomy of individuals. This includes respecting freedom, dignity, and privacy.

## Principle 3: Fairness

AI systems should be inclusive and accessible, and should not involve or result in unfair discrimination against individuals, communities, or groups. Throughout their lifecycle, AI systems should be inclusive and accessible, and should not involve or result in unfair discrimination. This includes taking measures to prevent and mitigate against biases.

## Principle 4: Privacy Protection and Security

AI systems should respect and uphold privacy rights and data protection, and ensure the security of data. Throughout their lifecycle, AI systems should respect and uphold privacy rights and data protection. Personal data should only be collected, used, disclosed, and stored as necessary, with appropriate safeguards.

## Principle 5: Reliability and Safety

AI systems should reliably operate in accordance with their intended purpose. Throughout their lifecycle, AI systems should reliably operate in accordance with their intended purpose. They should be robust, secure, and safe throughout their entire lifecycle.

## Principle 6: Transparency and Explainability

There should be transparency and responsible disclosure so people can understand when they are being significantly impacted by AI, and can find out when an AI system is engaging with them. People should be able to understand the AI system's processes and how decisions are made.

## Principle 7: Contestability

When an AI system significantly impacts a person, community, group, or environment, there should be a timely process to allow people to challenge the use or outcomes of the AI system. Effective mechanisms should be in place for redress.

## Principle 8: Accountability

People responsible for the different phases of the AI system lifecycle should be identifiable and accountable for the outcomes of the AI systems, and human oversight of AI systems should be enabled. Organisations developing or deploying AI should establish clear lines of accountability.

---

## Application to SMEs

Small and medium enterprises should:
- Designate an AI governance contact responsible for AI ethics compliance
- Conduct regular reviews of AI tool usage against these principles
- Maintain transparency about AI use with customers and stakeholders
- Ensure human oversight of AI-assisted decisions
- Document AI systems in use and their purposes
- Provide staff training on ethical AI use
