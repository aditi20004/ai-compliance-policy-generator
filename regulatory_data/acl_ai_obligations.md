# Australian Consumer Law (ACL) and AI-Generated Content

## Overview

The Treasury's review of AI and the Australian Consumer Law (October 2025) concluded that the ACL's principles-based framework is broadly fit-for-purpose for AI-enabled goods and services. This means existing obligations apply NOW — no new legislation is needed.

## Key ACL Obligations for SMEs Using AI

### Section 18: Misleading or Deceptive Conduct (Strict Liability)

- **No intent required** — if AI generates false information and your business publishes it, you may contravene the ACL
- If an SME deploys an AI chatbot that makes false representations about products, pricing, or consumer rights, the business is liable
- The ACCC has specifically warned that AI chatbots "may not accurately communicate consumer guarantee rights under Australian law"
- AI-generated fake reviews have grown by **over 1,000%** on certain platforms (2022–2025) — Tier 1 ACCC enforcement concern

### Consumer Guarantees (Sections 54–56)

- **Acceptable quality** (s54): AI-enabled products must work properly. Systematically incorrect or biased AI outputs may not meet this standard
- **Fitness for purpose** (s55): If you market an AI tool as suitable for a specific purpose, it must deliver on that promise
- **Match description** (s56): AI-generated product descriptions must accurately describe the product

### ACCC AI Industry Snapshot (December 2025)

The ACCC's 55-page report identified emerging risks:
1. **Autonomous agent systems** — including collusion and evidence challenges
2. **Privacy violations** through systematic data collection
3. **"AI washing"** — companies making misleading claims about AI functionality
4. Fake AI-generated reviews and testimonials

## Specific Scenarios for SMEs

### AI Chatbots
- Must not make false claims about products, services, or consumer rights
- Must clearly disclose that the customer is interacting with AI
- Must have human escalation for consumer guarantee issues
- AI hallucinations about product features = misleading conduct

### AI-Generated Marketing Content
- All AI-generated product claims must be verifiable
- Testimonials and reviews must be genuine — AI-generated reviews are prohibited
- AI-generated images must accurately represent the product
- Disclaimers about AI use in content generation recommended

### AI-Generated Pricing
- Automated pricing algorithms must not engage in price fixing (even unintentionally via algorithmic collusion)
- Dynamic pricing must be transparent
- AI-driven discounts must be honoured as advertised

### AI-Generated Financial Advice
- AI tools cannot provide personalised financial advice without appropriate licences
- General information vs financial advice distinction must be maintained
- AI-generated projections must include appropriate disclaimers

## Compliance Requirements for SMEs

1. **Review all AI-generated customer-facing content** before publication
2. **Implement human oversight** for AI chatbot interactions
3. **Verify accuracy** of AI-generated product descriptions and claims
4. **Document AI involvement** in content creation processes
5. **Train staff** to identify potentially misleading AI outputs
6. **Maintain complaint handling** procedures for AI-related consumer issues
7. **Do not use AI to generate fake reviews** or testimonials
8. **Disclose AI use** where it materially affects the consumer interaction

## Penalties

ACL penalties align with broader competition law:
- Maximum penalties for corporations: the greater of $50 million, three times the benefit, or 30% of turnover
- Individual penalties up to $2.5 million
- Plus consumer remedies (refunds, compensation, injunctions)
